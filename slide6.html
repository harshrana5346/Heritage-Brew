Ancient Indian Drinks (Heritage Base)
A quick carousel through traditional beverages — their ancient role and modern relevance.

Soma

Ancient base: A sacred ritual drink consumed in Vedic ceremonies and symbol of divine communion.
Modern relevance: Primarily mytho-ritual; inspires beverages that celebrate ritual and purity.

Sura

Ancient base: Grain-based fermented drink (barley, rice, millet, jaggery) commonly consumed for pleasure and strength.
Modern relevance: Survives as Handia/Chhang; great base for millet-craft beverages and probiotic versions.

Parisrut

Ancient base: A stronger fermented grain mash noted in texts like the Atharvaveda.
Modern relevance: Parallels in tribal strong millet/rice beers — ideal for premium heritage brews.

Madhu (Mead)

Ancient base: Honey-fermented wine (mead), associated with ritual and sweetness.
Modern relevance: Revivable as craft mead (alcoholic) and non-alcoholic honey wellness drinks.

Maireya

Ancient base: Jaggery and spice brews, festive and spiced for community consumption.
Modern relevance: Perfect for spiced mocktail syrups, mixers, and light festive beverages.

Asava & Arishta

Ancient base: Ayurvedic fermented herbal tonics used for digestion and stamina.
Modern relevance: Still sold as Ayurvedic tonics — excellent for premium wellness branding.

Varuni

Ancient base: Mythical liquor of goddess Varuni (linked to Samudra Manthan) — symbolic of sacred intoxication.
Modern relevance: Inspires branding and storytelling for palm-based drinks and toddy traditions.

Kilāla

Ancient base: Lesser-known fermented grain drink cited in traditional lists.
Modern relevance: Opportunity for niche craft revivals and micro-regional branding.

Harahuraka

Ancient base: Another traditional fermented grain beverage with ritual and communal roles.
Modern relevance: Culturally rich, ideal for storytelling-driven craft beverages.

Ayasthana

Ancient base: Fermented grain drink appearing in classical sources.
Modern relevance: Adds depth to a heritage product range and regional narratives.
