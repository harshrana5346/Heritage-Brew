Role of Drinks in India — Ancient to Modern

Timeline of major eras and how drinks influenced ritual life, livelihoods, and state revenue.

Vedic Age — Soma, Sura & Madhu

Drinks were central to rituals and sacred ceremonies. Production tied to priestly rites and seasonal harvests.

Economic role: ceremonial value; limited market exchange; communal sharing tied to rituals and seasonal redistribution.

Mauryan Period — Arthashastra references

State control and taxation start appearing in texts; liquor was taxed and regulated.

Economic role: taxation becomes a formal revenue source; state policing of production and trade begins.

Medieval India — Distillation & cultural drinks

Distillation techniques spread; local specialty drinks (arrack, toddy, feni) become regionally important.

Economic role: growth of local industries, craft-makers, regional trade and artisan livelihoods.

Colonial Era — Excise & European liquors

Colonial excise policies reshaped markets; imported liquors gain dominance, local brews decline in many regions.

Economic role: excise becomes a major colonial revenue stream; local producers face competition and regulation.

Modern Period — Industry & livelihoods

Alcohol industry becomes a key source of state revenue; local traditional drinks survive in pockets and see revival interest.

Economic role: large-scale industry, state excise revenue, employment in brewing/distilling; revival potential for heritage beverages.
