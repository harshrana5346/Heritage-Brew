The Problem
India had a rich heritage of beverages (Soma, Sura, Madhu, Asava, Parisrut). Today many traditions are vanishing — replaced by foreign soft drinks and modern alcohol; health, culture and sustainability are at risk.

Heritage fading

Traditional beverages and tribal practices are disappearing, threatening cultural identity.

Plastic pollution

Plastic bottles dominate packaging, adding to pollution and harming sustainability goals.

Foreign soft drinks

Imported sodas and alcohol brands dominate markets, sidelining Indian heritage drinks.

Health concerns

Rising lifestyle diseases are linked with sugary, processed drinks and unhealthy habits.

Vanishing Traditions

Ancient beverages like Soma, Sura, Madhu, Asava and Parisrut are fading as urban markets shift and knowledge transmission declines.

Market Flooded by Foreign Brands

Global soft drink and alcohol brands dominate mainstream shelves, reducing visibility for local heritage drinks.

Health & Packaging Issues

Processed soft drinks and single-use plastic packaging increase health concerns and environmental waste.

Opportunity

Rising demand for organic, sustainable and traditional beverages creates a market window for Heritage Brews to revive and scale heritage drinks.
